Privacy Policy

Last Updated: February 17, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how CloudMobile ("we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, stores, and protects your information when you use the Happy Habits mobile application ("the App"). We are committed to helping you build better routines while protecting your personal data.

By using Happy Habits, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree with this policy, please do not use the App.

1. Information We Collect

We collect information to provide a personalized habit-tracking experience and to improve app performance.

1.1 Habit and Progress Data

The App is designed to track your daily routines. We store the information you manually enter:

  • Habit Names and Descriptions: The titles and goals of the habits you wish to build.
  • Check-in Logs: Dates and times when you complete a habit.
  • Measurable Values: Numeric data for measurable habits (e.g., "8 glasses of water," "5 kilometers run").
  • Performance Metrics: Calculated data such as streaks, completion rates, and historical scores.

1.2 Device and Technical Information

We automatically collect certain technical information for stability and core functionality:

  • Device type and Operating System (Android version).
  • App version and Package Name.
  • Device identifiers (required for scheduling reminders and exact alarms).
  • IP address (used generally for regional compliance and security).

1.3 Advertising Information

Happy Habits uses Google AdMob to display advertisements. AdMob may collect:

  • Advertising ID: A unique, user-resettable ID for advertising, used to provide personalized ads.
  • Interaction Data: Information about ad views and clicks.

You can manage your ad preferences or reset your Advertising ID in your Android system settings.

1.4 Diagnostic and Analytics Data

To improve app stability, we collect anonymized crash reports and usage patterns to understand which features are most helpful to our users.

2. How We Use Your Information

2.1 To Provide the Service

  • To display your habit progress, streaks, and statistics.
  • To fire reminders at your selected times (using Exact Alarm permissions).
  • To calculate "Atomic" vs "Elastic" scores based on your logged progress.

2.2 To Improve the App

  • To fix bugs and prevent app crashes.
  • To analyze which habit frequencies (Daily, Weekly, etc.) are most commonly used to optimize the interface.

2.3 Legal Compliance

  • To comply with regional privacy laws like GDPR (EU) and CCPA (California) via the integrated Google User Messaging Platform (UMP).

3. Data Storage and Security

Local-First Storage: By default, Happy Habits stores your habit data locally on your device in a secure SQLite database. We do not currently use a central cloud server to sync your personal habits; your data stays on your device.

3.1 Backups and Portability

The App provides features for you to control your data:

  • JSON Export/Import: You can create a full backup of your habits and history to move to a new device.
  • CSV Export: You can export your data as a spreadsheet for your own personal analysis.

3.2 Security

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect your data stored within the App. However, please remember that no method of electronic storage is 100% secure.

5. Your Rights and Data Control

5.1 Deletion

You have full control over your data. You can delete individual habits, clear all history, or use the "Reset All Data" option in the Settings menu to permanently erase all App information from your device.

5.2 Access and Portability

You can view all logs via the Calendar and Statistics screens. You can export your entire database at any time using the Export features in Settings.

5.3 Regional Rights

Users in the European Economic Area (EEA) and California are prompted with a consent form upon first launch to manage their data sharing preferences for personalized advertising, in compliance with GDPR and CCPA.

6. Children's Privacy

Happy Habits is intended for general audiences. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If we discover a child under 13 has provided us with personal data, we immediately delete this from our records.

We may update our Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new Privacy Policy on this page and updating the "Last Updated" date.
